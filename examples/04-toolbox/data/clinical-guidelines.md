# Clinical Practice Guidelines — Summary Document

## 1. Hypertension Management (2024 Updated Guidelines)

### Diagnosis
- Hypertension is defined as systolic blood pressure (SBP) ≥ 130 mmHg or diastolic blood pressure (DBP) ≥ 80 mmHg.
- Diagnosis requires elevated readings on at least two separate occasions.
- Ambulatory blood pressure monitoring (ABPM) is recommended to confirm diagnosis and rule out white-coat hypertension.

### Risk Stratification
| Risk Level | Criteria |
|------------|----------|
| Low | Stage 1 hypertension, no organ damage, 10-year CVD risk < 10% |
| Moderate | Stage 1 hypertension with 1-2 risk factors, 10-year CVD risk 10-20% |
| High | Stage 2 hypertension, or any stage with organ damage, diabetes, or CVD risk > 20% |

### Treatment Targets
- General population: < 130/80 mmHg
- Patients aged > 65 years: SBP 130-139 mmHg if tolerated
- Patients with CKD and proteinuria: < 130/80 mmHg
- Pregnant patients: < 135/85 mmHg

### First-line Medications
1. **ACE inhibitors** (e.g., ramipril, lisinopril) — preferred in diabetes, heart failure, CKD
2. **ARBs** (e.g., losartan, valsartan) — alternative to ACE inhibitors if cough develops
3. **Calcium channel blockers** (e.g., amlodipine) — preferred in elderly, Black patients
4. **Thiazide diuretics** (e.g., indapamide, chlorthalidone) — effective add-on therapy

### Combination Therapy
- Most patients require ≥ 2 agents to achieve target.
- Preferred combinations: ACE/ARB + CCB, ACE/ARB + diuretic.
- Avoid: ACE + ARB combination (increased adverse effects, no benefit).

## 2. Type 2 Diabetes Management (2024 Consensus)

### Diagnostic Criteria
- Fasting plasma glucose ≥ 7.0 mmol/L (126 mg/dL)
- HbA1c ≥ 48 mmol/mol (6.5%)
- 2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L (200 mg/dL) during OGTT
- Random plasma glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L with symptoms

### Glycaemic Targets
| Population | HbA1c Target |
|-----------|-------------|
| Most adults | < 53 mmol/mol (7.0%) |
| Newly diagnosed, low hypoglycaemia risk | < 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) |
| Elderly or high comorbidity burden | < 64 mmol/mol (8.0%) |
| Pregnancy (pre-existing T2DM) | < 43 mmol/mol (6.1%) |

### Pharmacological Stepwise Approach
1. **Step 1**: Metformin (first-line for all patients unless contraindicated)
2. **Step 2**: Add SGLT2 inhibitor (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) — especially if heart failure or CKD present
3. **Step 3**: Add GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide, liraglutide) — especially if obesity or atherosclerotic CVD
4. **Step 4**: Consider insulin therapy if HbA1c remains above target despite dual/triple oral therapy

### Cardiovascular Risk Reduction
- All patients with T2DM and established CVD should receive SGLT2i or GLP-1 RA regardless of HbA1c.
- Statin therapy recommended for all patients aged > 40 years.
- Aspirin 75 mg daily for secondary prevention only (not routine primary prevention).

## 3. Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) Screening and Management

### Staging
| Stage | GFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | Description |
|-------|---------------------|-------------|
| G1 | ≥ 90 | Normal or high |
| G2 | 60-89 | Mildly decreased |
| G3a | 45-59 | Mild to moderate decrease |
| G3b | 30-44 | Moderate to severe decrease |
| G4 | 15-29 | Severely decreased |
| G5 | < 15 | Kidney failure |

### Albuminuria Categories
- A1: < 3 mg/mmol (normal)
- A2: 3-30 mg/mmol (moderately increased)
- A3: > 30 mg/mmol (severely increased)

### Key Interventions
- **RAAS blockade**: ACE inhibitor or ARB for all patients with albuminuria (A2 or A3).
- **SGLT2 inhibitors**: Recommended for CKD G2-G4 with albuminuria (proven to slow progression).
- **Finerenone**: Non-steroidal MRA for T2DM with CKD — reduces composite kidney/CV endpoint.
- **Blood pressure target**: < 130/80 mmHg.
- **Avoid NSAIDs**: Nephrotoxic, especially in G3-G5.
- **Referral to nephrology**: GFR < 30, rapid decline (> 5 mL/min/year), or A3 albuminuria.

## 4. Lipid Management and Statin Therapy

### Who Should Receive a Statin?
- All patients with established atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD)
- All patients with T2DM aged > 40 years
- Patients with 10-year ASCVD risk ≥ 10% (QRISK3 or Pooled Cohort Equations)
- Patients with familial hypercholesterolaemia

### LDL-C Targets
| Risk Category | LDL-C Target |
|--------------|-------------|
| Very high risk (established CVD, recurrent events) | < 1.4 mmol/L and ≥ 50% reduction |
| High risk (single CVD event, DM with organ damage) | < 1.8 mmol/L and ≥ 50% reduction |
| Moderate risk (T2DM without organ damage, CKD G3) | < 2.6 mmol/L |
| Low risk (primary prevention, no risk factors) | < 3.0 mmol/L |

### Agents
1. **High-intensity statins** (atorvastatin 40-80 mg, rosuvastatin 20-40 mg) — first-line
2. **Ezetimibe** (10 mg) — add-on if target not met on maximum tolerated statin
3. **PCSK9 inhibitors** (evolocumab, alirocumab) — for very high risk patients not at target despite statin + ezetimibe
4. **Bempedoic acid** — alternative for statin-intolerant patients
5. **Inclisiran** — siRNA therapy, given every 6 months by injection

### Monitoring
- Repeat lipid profile 8-12 weeks after starting/changing therapy.
- Check liver function (ALT) at baseline and 12 weeks.
- CK measurement only if myalgia symptoms develop.

## 5. Anticoagulation in Atrial Fibrillation

### CHA₂DS₂-VASc Score
| Factor | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Congestive heart failure | 1 |
| Hypertension | 1 |
| Age ≥ 75 | 2 |
| Diabetes | 1 |
| Stroke/TIA/thromboembolism | 2 |
| Vascular disease | 1 |
| Age 65-74 | 1 |
| Sex category (female) | 1 |

### Anticoagulation Recommendations
- Score ≥ 2 (men) or ≥ 3 (women): Anticoagulate with DOAC.
- Score = 1 (men) or 2 (women): Consider anticoagulation (discuss with patient).
- Score = 0 (men) or 1 (women): No anticoagulation needed.

### Direct Oral Anticoagulants (DOACs) — Preferred Over Warfarin
| Drug | Standard Dose | Renal Dose | Contraindicated |
|------|--------------|------------|-----------------|
| Apixaban | 5 mg BD | 2.5 mg BD (if ≥ 2 of: age ≥ 80, weight ≤ 60 kg, Cr ≥ 133) | Mechanical valve, severe MS |
| Rivaroxaban | 20 mg OD | 15 mg OD (CrCl 15-49) | CrCl < 15 |
| Edoxaban | 60 mg OD | 30 mg OD (CrCl 15-50, weight ≤ 60 kg) | CrCl < 15 |
| Dabigatran | 150 mg BD | 110 mg BD (age ≥ 80 or verapamil) | CrCl < 30 |

### Key Points
- DOACs are preferred over warfarin for non-valvular AF.
- No routine INR monitoring required with DOACs.
- Renal function should be checked annually (more frequently if CrCl < 60).
- Do NOT combine anticoagulant with antiplatelet unless recent ACS/PCI (time-limited).

## 6. Asthma Management (Stepwise Approach)

### Step 1 — Mild Intermittent
- As-needed low-dose ICS-formoterol (preferred)
- Alternative: SABA as needed (no longer recommended as sole therapy)

### Step 2 — Mild Persistent
- Low-dose ICS-formoterol as maintenance and reliever (MART)
- Alternative: Low-dose ICS + as-needed SABA

### Step 3 — Moderate
- Medium-dose ICS-formoterol (MART)
- Alternative: Low-dose ICS-LABA + as-needed SABA

### Step 4 — Moderate-Severe
- Medium/high-dose ICS-LABA
- Add LAMA (tiotropium) if uncontrolled
- Consider referral for biologics assessment

### Step 5 — Severe
- Refer to specialist
- Consider biologic therapy: anti-IgE (omalizumab), anti-IL5 (mepolizumab), anti-IL4/13 (dupilumab)
- Low-dose oral corticosteroids as last resort

### Key Principles
- SABA-only treatment is no longer recommended at any step.
- All patients should have an ICS-containing controller.
- Over-use of SABA (≥ 3 canisters/year) is a marker of poor control and increased exacerbation risk.
- Review inhaler technique and adherence before stepping up.
